\subsection{Database Design}

In data model design, an Entity–Relationship Diagram (ERD) is used to capture the conceptual model of a coding challenge and online judging system. It visualizes the core entities of the platform—such as users, problems, submissions, test cases, and judging results—and the relationships among them. The ERD serves a similar purpose to a UML class diagram, but at a higher level of abstraction. Therefore, including both diagrams would be redundant, and the ERD alone is sufficient for representing the system’s conceptual data structure.
